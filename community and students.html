The Academic Formation Program

Academic Standards
  1. Curriculum. The school follows the curriculum prescribed and approved by the Department of Education. There are however, added institutional programs by the School by its status as Catholic institution which are pertinent in the realization of the School's Vision, Mission and Objectives.

  2. Academic Policies. The Diocese of Baguio-Schools, in its pursuit of Life transforming and Christ disciple forming educational communities adopts the CHILD CENTERED LEARNING FRAMEWORK. Putting the framework into action leads to the realization of the DOB-S vision of a life transforming and Christ-disciple forming educational communities that produce individuals imbued with INTEGRITY, EXCELLENCE, SERVANT LEADERSHIP and CULTURE SENSITIVITY action requires th whole school and school system transformation supporte by the key principles of child-centered learning.

    3. a. Learning happens anytime anywhere
    - Students learn outside the classroom, outside the traditional school walls and beyond the traditional school days.
    - Knowledge is not a given truth, but a result action or experimentation.

    b. Learning is personalized
    - Presence of strong relationship between teacher and learner that enhances learners' self-worth, self-confidenc and motivations.
    - A supportive atmosphere without fear of being reproach embarrassed or reprimanded.
    Personalized learning recognizes that students engage in different ways and in different places. Students benefit from individually paced, targeted learning tasks that formatively assess existing skills and knowledge and that address the student's needs and interests.

    c. Learning is outcomes-based
    - Uncovering of facts, concepts and understanding in interdisciplinary context.
    - Exploring multiple viewpoints through interacting collaborating, inquiring, self-assessing and reflecting Integrating of the PVMOCV. where facts and procedures are used
    - Learning experiences in the context of solving of a real life problem.
    - Outcomes-based and authentic performance task.
    Students move ahead when they have demonstrated certain mastery of content, not when they've reached a birthday or undergone the required hours in a classroom.

    d. Learners take ownership of their own learning
    - Learners take a direct role in their own learning and success
    - Learners are led towards creative and critical analysis through their engagement to meaningful learning activities
    - Engages students in their own success and incorporates their interests and skills into the learning process. Learners support one another's progress and celebrate success.
    - Periodical tests, unit tests, quizzes, oral and written reports, as well as assignments and performance tasks are necessary in order to grade you objectively; and for you to gauge your academic performance in school

  3. School Term. The academic year is based on the school calendar prescribed or approved by the Department of education.

  4. Attendance. Attendance is a personal presence in the school especially in the particularly place of instruction. The student's total educational growth and personal development depend to a large extent on regular school attendance.
